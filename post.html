  • Google Code-in 2016
    29 August 2017

    Hi! I am a Google Code-in (GCI) 2016 Grand Prize Winner. Programming was not the reason I liked opening my computer every day after school in third grade, it was playing games, but when I learned my first programming language, the first thing to be removed from my computer was games. I started programming in the seventh grade when I started learning Visual Basic. I liked the simplicity and the ease with which I could develop a usable software. My first piece of usable software was a controller software for a robot I had made. It was amazing to see that robot move around the room. I learned C in the ninth grade and have progressively learned new programming languages since then. Now, I have started to learn Go. The reason I am learning Go has a lot to do with GCI. I got a Gopher during the GCI Winner's Trip which I keep on my desk, and it has inspired me to learn Go! I first took part in GCI 2015 when I tried to do my first task in the last week of the contest. I could not complete that task because it took me a lot of time to get the hang of Qt.

    Why did it take me soooo much time? Even though I was never into open source coding, I have used open source software since the day I started using a computer. From Ubuntu to Firefox. I loved these amazing software but I never thought I could contribute to them. Once GCI 2015 was complete, I started learning about open source software and how I could contribute. I felt ready for GCI 2016!

    November 28, 2016, came too soon. I logged into my account and registered for the contest. I picked my first task and waited for ages for a file to download. My mouse shivering I clicked on submit the task. And voila! Task Accepted! Delight! I went to sleep quite happily with myself. The next day, another task! Voila! Done the same day! Next day, another task. Voila! Nah! This task took me a few days. It was a fantastic feeling to find that I had won a GCI t-shirt. I felt that I should try to do more tasks. It was extremely engrossing. You might ask, which organization did I work for? I worked for BRL-CAD. BRL-CAD is the oldest known public version-controlled codebase. Why did I choose it? Because I like CAD and I loved the history associated with this software.

    Over the next five weeks, I completed tasks ranging from design to code to debugging. Most of the coding tasks I did were related to web development, the area of computer programming I cannot get enough of. GCI got over pretty quickly for me. A few weeks after the contest, I was ready to go to school when I checked my mail to see if I had any new messages and hurrah! " You have been chosen as a GCI Grand Prize Winner!" I jumped with joy (and got late for school because I hurt my feet as I jumped in delight quite close to my desk). It was a fantastic feeling! I read the email pretty quickly and wanted to leave for the trip the next day. I could not help but smile for the rest of the day.

    My trip was in last week of June, and it was awesome! It was great to meet amazing students and coders from all over the world. The memories I made in the trip riding a Segway at full speed and getting sprayed on a yacht will live with me forever. I cannot help but relive the memories of the trip while typing this article. Now I will change tabs and learn error handling in Go while the Gopher looks over my laptop, inspiring me and reminding me of the experience that was Google Code-in 2016.
